routes
read more about our specially crafted routes
These are the safaris through the whole island to maximize the possible enjoyment from your voyage, carefully crafted adapting to both the season of the year and your interest when visiting here. We offer lodging, but if you so desire you can look for it yourself with or without our counselling. We also provide EVERY kind of tool needed to enjoy our routes, from binoculars to better observe birds to kits for our guided diving to see whales when it’s time, everything included.
Madagascar is just a quarter of the size of the Democratic Republic of Congo, yet boasts an incredible number of different lemur species, each of which is completely endemic. Most famous is the indri whose calls awaken weary travellers with a rush of excitement, other famous species include the ring tailed and dancing sifaka. Perhaps a little less known is the eerie aye aye, whose intriguing looks, including bulbous eye and a very long finger, led to much supernatural suspicion among the Malagasy people.
MEET THE AMAZING ENDEMIC LEMURS

DAY 1 - LEMUR’S ROUTE

On day 1 we will go through the forest of Ambatolampy and Moramanga to see the lemur’s habitat and how they interact with each other.

A variety of different lemur species inhabit the Red Island, from the dwindling forests in the interior to the scrublands of the south and even in the forests and beaches of Anjajavy. Experiencing these intriguing creatures is very different to trekking the mountain gorillas of Rwanda and Uganda, or orang-utans in Borneo, with such a range of species and personas to spot.

DAY 2 - LEMUR’S ROUTE

On day 2 we will stay the whole day at the forest near Ilakaka where resides over 20 different endemic species of birds, from wich 4 of them are unique to this forest in the whole island.

Our tour starts in Antananarivo to reach Ampijoroa, a must-visited site for all birders visiting Madagascar. It is included in Ankarafantsika Reserve, in the dry deciduous forest. This site gives a real snapshot of the western biome of Madagascar. The Lake Ravelobe, home for several aquatic birds also gives an extra dimension to the birdlife at Ankarafantsika.

DAY 3 - LEMUR’S ROUTE

On day 3 we’ll go from Antalaha troguh Sambava towards Vohemar on boat and we’ll watch how the whales start coming closer to the coasts.

It’s just off the eastern coast of Madagascar is the perfect spot to observe the humpback whales who visit the area from June to September. Not only is it a stunning beach paradise of white sands and palm fronds, as well as the location of a pirate cemetery dating from hundreds of years ago. Travellers can spot the cetaceans easily from the shore or venture out by boat with a responsible conservation-focused company.
